Professional Code of Ethics

Introduction

This Professional Code of Ethics (hereinafter referred to as the "Code") establishes behavioral standards and ethical norms for all members of the organization. Its purpose is to foster a culture of integrity, responsibility, and respect, while strengthening the organization's reputation as a reliable and consistent partner in the business community.

The Code is a mandatory document for review and signature by all organization participants, including cluster leaders and new members. Compliance with its provisions is an essential condition for participation in the organization's activities. The document publicly declares our values and principles, demonstrating commitment to high professional standards.

Core Principles

The organization's activities are based on the following fundamental values:

1. Integrity and Responsibility

Organization members must fulfill their promises and commitments, even when they become disadvantageous.

If conditions change, new arrangements must be agreed upon with partners or original commitments must be honored.

Every word must be backed by action. If feedback is promised, it must be provided on time, even if information is currently unavailable - in which case new deadlines should be specified.

2. Reputation and Honor

The reputation of the organization and its members is a priority. Each participant's actions should contribute to maintaining and enhancing the organization's good name.

We strive to be known as reliable partners whose words and actions are consistent.

3. Courtesy and Tact

Members must demonstrate respect and politeness in all interactions, avoiding arrogance, inaccessibility, and ostentatious ambition.

4. Feedback

Providing timely and constructive feedback is every member's obligation. Even when information is unavailable, members must notify others and specify when it will be available.

Behavior and Ethics

Organization members must maintain high standards of behavior both within the team and in public:

1. Public Conduct

Actions that could be perceived as offensive, disrespectful, or inappropriate are unacceptable.

Behavior in public places must align with the organization's reputation.

2. Internal and Partner Interactions

Members must avoid lobbying for their own interests or those of their companies at the expense of other participants.

Interactions should be based on principles of mutually beneficial cooperation, openness, and tact.

3. Compliance with Laws and Regulations

All members must comply with current legislation, internal organizational rules, and ethical standards.

Violations of these norms are considered serious offenses.

Accountability

Organization members bear significant responsibility for their actions:

1. Scope of Responsibility

As the organization's influence and transaction volume grow, leaders' responsibility increases.

Each participant must understand the scale of their role and be prepared for challenges.

2. Reputational Responsibility

Each member's behavior reflects on the entire organization's reputation. Inappropriate behavior in public or professional settings is unacceptable.

Organization members bear personal moral and reputational responsibility for their actions.

3. Self-Control and Awareness

Members must understand their limits and not avoid responsibility by citing task complexity or operational scale.

The organization expects high levels of self-discipline and consistency.

Consequences of Violations

Code violations result in strict measures:

1. Disciplinary Actions

When violations are identified, an internal investigation is conducted.

Depending on the violation's severity, measures may include warnings, temporary suspension, or expulsion.

Serious violations lead to immediate expulsion.

2. Public Censure

If a member's behavior damages the organization's reputation, their actions will be publicly condemned by leadership.

The organization will not defend members who violate the Code, regardless of their status or personal connections.

The club's reputation supersedes individual interests. Violators will be expelled and publicly censured.

Review and Signing Process

1. Review

All organization members, including new participants, must carefully review the Code.

The document is public and available to all interested parties.

2. Signing

Each participant must sign the Code, confirming agreement with its provisions and willingness to comply.

Signing is a mandatory requirement for joining and participating in the organization's activities.

3. Reminders and Training

The organization will regularly remind members of the Code's provisions and conduct training, especially for cluster leaders, on ethics and accountability.

Conclusion

The Professional Code of Ethics forms the foundation for creating a club of honor, conscience, word, responsibility, and respect. Adherence to its principles will enable us to become an example of impeccable professionalism, earn the business community's trust, and strengthen the organization's reputation as a reliable partner. We are a club of consistency and mutually beneficial cooperation where words always match deeds.
